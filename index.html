The Shift

The job is no longer the safe option.
The one-person company is.

For fifty years, the deal was simple: trade your time for stability. Show up, do the work, get the paycheck. The company would take care of the rest.

That deal is over.

Layoffs arrive in waves. Roles get reorganized into spreadsheets. Decades of expertise get replaced by an org chart redesign and a Slack message. The "safe" path now ends with a calendar invite and a severance letter.

At the same time, AI just collapsed the cost of building a real business. One person with the right systems can now do what used to take a 20-person team.

The alternative hypothesis: you stop waiting to be cut, and start building something they can't take away.
